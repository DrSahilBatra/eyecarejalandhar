Do I Need To Get My Eyes Checked If I Have Headache ?




Yes, an eye examination is necessary for a complete evaluation of causes leading to a headache. However, there are multiple reasons for headache and not just the eyes. Following are the causes of headache related to the eye:


Eye Strain

It is mainly caused by uncorrected refractive errors of the eye(improper focusing) and misalignment of eyes. Children with refractive errors usually do not complain of eyestrain as they have strong accommodative power. It usually occurs when we use our eyes for a long period of time- working on the computer, prolonged reading or near-work like sewing. Headache caused by eye-strain is usually relieved by prescription of glasses or correction of misalignment.


Headache which are not related to eye strain

Most primary headache disorder like tension headache, cluster headache or migraine are not related to eye-strain. These headaches usually occur during sleep or while awakening and are associated with nausea, vomiting or appearance of flashes of light in front of the eye. A neurologist consultation is required for proper treatment as they are not related to visual concerns.



Other eye problems which can cause headache


Cornea Related Problems

The front surface of the eye (cornea) is the most sensitive part of the eye which has a rich nerve supply. Even small dust particles or superficial scratch on the cornea can cause extreme pain, redness and watering in the eye which requires urgent medical care. Degenerative corneal diseases or infection also causes pain and redness in the eye.


Dry Eye

Dry eye can cause severe itching, burning, redness, excessive tearing, foreign body sensation or fluctuating vision. It is worsened by activities which require concentration leading to infrequent blinking of eyes like- working on computer, reading, T.V. watching or driving, exposure to hot or very cold temperatures. If you have such symptoms, you should consult your eye doctor.


Glaucoma

It is a progressive disease which can lead to permanent blindness. Mild eye pain and headache occur in patients with chronic angle-closure glaucoma, An acute attack of glaucoma can cause severe pain, redness and watering in the eye which requires urgent medical care. In conclusion, an eye examination is necessary if you have a headache related to prolonged working hours or have other eye symptoms like redness, watering in the eye.
